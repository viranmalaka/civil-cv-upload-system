Who we are ?

The department of Civil Engineering is the largest academic department in the campus which started its, postgraduate programmes as early as in 1976. with the assistance of UNESCO.

The department was founded in 1978 when katubedda campus was converted in to University of Moratuwa and is operated under 6 main divisions all of which includes post graduate and research programs. From 1985 the department is housed in its own building complex of around 8000 m2. The main building comprises of student lecture rooms, staff rooms, purpose built laboratories, drawing studios and an auditorium which can accommodate 300 people. The rock mechanics lab is located in a separate building with 3 lecture room facilities and the Civil Engineering Research Centre completed in 2011 houses 2 more lecture halls, the Environmental Engineering laboratory, a GIS Centre and a canteen.

The new 3-storied building for the UNESCO Madanjeet Singh Centre for South Asia Water Management (UMCSAWM) attached to the Department of Civil Engineering has just been completed at a location adjacent to the other buildings of the Department, funded by the South Asia Foundation (SAF), with the objective of promoting regional cooperation through South Asian Water Management Education.

Vision

To be a centre of excellence of higher learning, research and related activities with emphasis on national relevance, international recognition, innovation and creativity in Civil Engineering.

Mission

To develop educational programs that provide educational, research and professional experiences that enable our graduates to become leaders in their professional careers, to pursue excellence in research and to serve the profession, community and nation, and be competitive in the international arena.
